# teenage engineering — Experimental Type Source Notes

Original URL: https://teenage.engineering
Category scan: Experimental Type
Theme: light
North star: engineered precision against industrial gray

## Essence

teenage engineering turns experimental typography into industrial product
precision. The system does not use expressive type through loud color or heavy
ornament. Instead, it uses ultra-light custom typography, rigid neutral fields,
thin borders, square edges, and a single technical blue accent. The personality
comes from restraint, extreme font thinness, and a product-grid rhythm that
feels closer to an engineered catalog than to a normal ecommerce page.

## Typography

- `te-20`: body, product text, navigation, detailed descriptions.
- `te-40`: display and prominent headings.
- Main weights are `100`, `300`, and very light `400` use only when necessary.
- Important sizes: `13px`, `19px`, `24px`, `26px`, `40px`.
- Line heights are compact: `1.11`, `1.15`, `1.50`.

## Layout

- Full-width top bar with industrial clarity.
- Product images often sit on gray or near-black technical surfaces.
- Main canvas remains light and almost sterile.
- Product display grids are predictable, flat, and square.
- Content alternates image-left/text-right or the inverse.
- Spacing: `15px` element gaps, `66px` section gaps.
- Radius: `0px`; corners stay sharp.

## Components

- Navigation links: `13px`, thin `te-20`, black on light canvas, blue on hover.
- Product grid item: product heading at `19px`, buy link at `13px`, blue CTA.
- Promotional banner: graphite background, white text/icons.
- Footer link: steel text on graphite background.
- Cookie banner: deliberately strange triangular yellow/black legal object,
  useful as evidence that experimental type can include one odd artifact.

## Reusable Lessons

- Make typography whisper, then give the layout strong geometry.
- Let one accent behave like a technical indicator light.
- Use flat surfaces and border systems instead of shadow.
- Use product imagery as the visual event, not decorative gradients.
- Never add radius unless the source demands it.

## Anti-Patterns

- Do not use heavy display weights.
- Do not spread Electric Blue into decorative areas.
- Do not add soft SaaS shadows or cards.
- Do not round product tiles.
- Do not crowd thin type; the letterforms need air.
