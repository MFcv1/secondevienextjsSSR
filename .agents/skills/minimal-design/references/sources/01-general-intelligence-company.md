# General Intelligence Company - Minimal Design Source Notes

## Core read

General Intelligence Company is the "architectural minimal authority"
archetype: a dark, atmospheric hero gives gravitas, then the page moves into a
predominantly minimalist, architectural light UI. It uses restrained typography,
achromatic surfaces, a single cool blue accent, and gentle translucent layering.

This is useful for minimal design because it shows that minimal does not have
to be blank. A single atmospheric moment can introduce ambition, while the rest
of the system stays calm, precise, and content-led.

## Typography

- Serif for major headings, used sparingly.
- Clean sans for body and functional UI.
- Headline voice is restrained and elegant.
- Do not over-bold.
- Let gravitas come from spacing and contrast, not heavy weight.

## Structure

- Full-bleed dark hero with centered headline.
- Contained 1200px-ish body sections.
- Alternating white/off-white backgrounds.
- Two-column text/image sections.
- Centered text stacks for emphasis.
- Three-column feature grids when needed.
- Compact sticky top bar.

## Minimal lessons

1. One atmospheric opening is enough
   - Dark hero can carry ambition.
   - Body sections should stay architectural and calm.

2. Blue accent is role-based
   - Active states, featured cards, key links.
   - Do not scatter blue decoration.

3. Shadows are gentle
   - Soft multi-layer shadows create depth without heaviness.
   - Avoid dramatic elevation.

4. Imagery is explanatory
   - Dark cityscape/illustrative hero is mood.
   - Content graphics are geometric, contained, and subdued.

5. Typography creates authority
   - Serif headline plus sans body creates calm intelligence.

## Anti-patterns

- Multiple atmospheric dark sections.
- Large colorful gradients in body sections.
- Heavy cards.
- Overusing blue accent.
- Decorative imagery where product explanation is needed.
- Dense UI without whitespace.
