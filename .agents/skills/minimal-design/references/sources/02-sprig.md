# Sprig - Minimal Design Source Notes

## Core read

Sprig is the "white canvas, thoughtful function" archetype: a soft minimalist
SaaS system where hierarchy is clear, surfaces are gentle, and warmth appears
through subtle gradients rather than saturated UI colors. The palette is mostly
achromatic and navy-led, with decorative gradients reserved for product
showcases or section warmth.

This source is important because it defines the softer side of minimal design:
not stark, not cold, but also not decorative. Function remains the center.

## Typography

- Compact and precise sans typography.
- High information density inside modules.
- Generous vertical spacing outside modules.
- Headings stay grounded in deep navy.
- Body copy should use slate, but not too pale.

## Structure

- Light canvas.
- Product cards and screenshots do the visual work.
- Large radii on imagery add softness.
- Borders are muted.
- Section spacing is airy.
- Saturated colors are avoided in functional UI.

## Minimal lessons

1. Soft minimal is still structured
   - Use clear hierarchy and precise components.
   - Do not rely on gradients alone for differentiation.

2. Gradients are atmosphere, not action
   - Warm/cool gradients live in imagery and section backgrounds.
   - CTAs remain charcoal or neutral.

3. Navy grounds the palette
   - Deep navy gives authority on a soft canvas.

4. Spacing separates modules
   - Air outside, density inside.

5. Large radius can soften without becoming playful
   - Use on images and cards, not every tiny UI element.

## Anti-patterns

- Saturated functional colors.
- Dark full sections beyond small contrast moments.
- Pastel text.
- Overly decorative gradients.
- Weak CTA contrast.
- Minimalism that becomes empty.
