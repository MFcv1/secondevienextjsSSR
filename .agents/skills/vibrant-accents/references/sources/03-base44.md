# Base44 - Vibrant Accents Source Notes

## Core read

Base44 is the softly lit accent archetype. It uses an off-white canvas, pastel
gradients, rounded interactions, and sharp pops of lime/orange. The color is
optimistic but not loud. The page feels like a creative AI workspace where
vibrant accents are signals layered over a soft, expansive foundation.

This is the gentlest version of Vibrant Accents: vibrant color is present, but
mostly as borders, selected states, hero gradients, and brand iconography.

## Typography

- Small utility: Arial-like, 10-14px.
- Display: custom/system fallback, 22-63px, weight 400.
- Section headings: custom/system fallback, 16-42px.
- Body/UI: Wix Madefor Text-like, 12-18px.
- Body letter spacing: positive, around 0.1em in extracted data.
- Display line-height ranges from 0.9 to 1.4.
- Overall: open, soft, not compressed.

## Structure

- Base unit: 4px.
- Density: comfortable.
- Section gap: 45px.
- Card padding: 40px.
- Element gap: 10px.
- Small radius: 3px.
- Card radius: about 7.42px.
- Default radius: about 9.9px.
- Prominent elements: about 13.85px.
- Buttons/tags: 999px or 300px pill-like radius.

## Component lessons

1. Lime border CTA
   - Lime Spritz as border/action signal.
   - Not necessarily a full fill.
   - Keeps the soft canvas intact.

2. Ghost pill button
   - Ash Border.
   - 999px radius.
   - Secondary actions feel approachable.

3. White workspace card
   - Snowdrift White over Canvas Pearl.
   - Soft contrast, not stark.

4. Pastel hero gradient
   - Low saturation.
   - Broad atmospheric use.
   - Should never become neon background noise.

5. Orange illustration accent
   - Brand iconography and decoration.
   - Not a competing CTA color.

## Design lessons

- Vibrance can be soft and airy.
- Lime can act as a precise signal without filling large areas.
- Rounded interactions make AI/workspace products feel approachable.
- Pastel gradients should support optimism, not dominate readability.
- Slightly unusual radii can add identity.

## Anti-patterns

- Do not use saturated backgrounds for large sections.
- Do not use sharp corners on interactive elements.
- Do not let orange and lime compete as equal CTAs.
- Do not place dark text on non-light backgrounds.
- Do not turn pastel gradients into neon gradients.
- Do not over-densify the layout.
