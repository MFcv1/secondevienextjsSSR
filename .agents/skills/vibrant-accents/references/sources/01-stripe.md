# Stripe - Vibrant Accents Source Notes

## Core read

Stripe is the controlled-vibrance archetype: a clean white command-center canvas
with a strong violet action color, subtle blue-gray product surfaces, and
abstract gradients used for hero/product showcase atmosphere. It proves that
vibrant accents can coexist with serious fintech/product clarity when the
accent roles are strict.

The main trick is hierarchy: the violet is primary interaction, related violets
support outlines and decoration, green/orange appear only as outline accents,
and gradients are visual anchors rather than a background treatment for every
section.

## Typography

- Primary: sohne-var or system-ui fallback.
- Weights: 300 and 400.
- Display: 56px at weight 300, tight line-height around 1.03.
- Large display tracking: around -0.03em.
- 48px tracking: around -0.025em.
- Body: 14-16px, weight 400.
- OpenType: stylistic set and tabular numbers.
- Important: large type stays light. Vibrance comes from color and gradients,
  not heavy headline weight.

## Structure

- Base unit: 4px.
- Density: comfortable.
- Section gap: 64px.
- Card padding: 12px.
- Element gap: 8px.
- Buttons/inputs/images/tags: 4px radius.
- Cards: 6px radius.
- Soft elevation: diffused shadows, not hard drop shadows.

## Component lessons

1. Deep Violet filled button
   - Fill #533afd.
   - White text.
   - 4px radius.
   - Primary action only.

2. Violet outline button
   - Transparent fill.
   - Deep Violet text.
   - Washed Violet border.
   - 4px radius.

3. Powder Blue card
   - #e5edf5 fill.
   - 6px radius.
   - Lightweight product container.

4. Gradient hero/product panel
   - Use violet/pink/light blue gradients.
   - Keep content readable and structured.
   - Do not cover every section in gradients.

5. Outline micro accents
   - Green/orange are for tags, dividers, focus edges.
   - They should not become additional CTA colors.

## Design lessons

- Vibrant accents can be sophisticated if the neutral system is quiet.
- Use one primary action hue and reserve siblings for support.
- Gradients should frame product meaning, not replace layout.
- Light font weights make saturated color feel more premium.
- Small radii can keep vibrant systems from becoming childish.

## Anti-patterns

- Do not use saturated colors for large text blocks.
- Do not make every accent a button color.
- Do not add new typefaces.
- Do not use generic blue actions.
- Do not use hard shadows.
- Do not over-round Stripe-like cards.
