# mymind - Expressive Brand Source Notes

## Core read

mymind is the "private memory editorial" archetype: warm orange gradient
atmosphere, serif display headlines, uppercase labels, pastel feature surfaces,
and a restrained product UI. The expressiveness comes from the tension between
emotional editorial language and a quiet personal-productivity product.

The core lesson: expressive brand does not require many colors. A single warm
accent can become a complete identity if it owns navigation dots, eyebrow
labels, outline buttons, gradient atmosphere, and section rhythm.

## Typography

- Display: Louize-like editorial serif.
- Display may use regular and italic variants.
- Body/UI: Open Sans or similar clean sans.
- Labels: Nunito-like uppercase with strong positive tracking.
- Eyebrows: uppercase, orange, +0.063em to +0.125em tracking.
- Display headings use aggressive negative tracking.
- The rhythm comes from contrast: lyrical serif versus spaced utility labels.

## Structure

- Light canvas with warm radial hero bloom.
- Product screenshot frames use 12-16px radius and thin gray border.
- Pastel feature cards elevate through color, not shadow.
- Orange is outline/mark/label, not solid CTA fill.
- Dark manifesto section can interrupt the light canvas for emotional contrast.

## Expressive lessons

1. Single-accent identity
   - Orange owns labels, dots, outlines, and atmosphere.
   - Do not add many bright colors.

2. Editorial voice for product memory
   - Serif headlines make utility feel personal.
   - Product UI remains clean and framed.

3. Navigation can be branded
   - Small orange bullets become wayfinding.
   - Brand appears in micro details, not only hero.

4. Feature tints replace shadows
   - Pastel surfaces differentiate concepts.
   - Avoid heavy card elevation.

5. Motion can be unhurried
   - Slow decelerating reveals match private, reflective positioning.

## Anti-patterns

- Filled orange CTA.
- Many chromatic accents.
- Generic sans-only typography.
- Shadows on pastel feature cards.
- Fast mechanical motion.
- Product screenshots with heavy device mockups.
