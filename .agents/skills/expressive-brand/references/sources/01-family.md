# Family - Expressive Brand Source Notes

## Core read

Family is the "illustrated fintech storybook" archetype: a warm cream canvas,
flat expressive mascots, vivid primary colors, and serious UI structure under
the playfulness. The identity is not just the palette. The illustrated
characters are the brand system: blob forms, stick limbs, dot eyes, simple
smiles, and object props that make a financial/crypto product feel human and
adventurous.

This reference is critical for expressive-brand work because it proves that a
highly playful identity can still feel competent when typography, spacing,
cards, and CTA contrast remain disciplined.

## Typography

- Display: custom Family typeface.
- Display fallback: Fraunces or Playfair Display at weight 500.
- Display sizes: 44px and 68px only.
- Display tracking: tight, around -0.031em at hero scale.
- UI/body: Inter/system, weights 400-600.
- Large Inter headings also use negative tracking.
- Keep display type rare: 2-3 major moments per page.

## Structure

- Base unit: 4px.
- Max width: about 1200px.
- Section gap: 120-180px.
- Card padding: 32px.
- Element gap: 8-12px.
- Card radius: 10px.
- Large card/phone mockup radius: 24px.
- Pill buttons: 32px radius.
- Illustration containers: 72px radius.

## Expressive lessons

1. Illustration is the identity
   - Characters are not decoration.
   - They define the brand vocabulary.
   - Each character claims one dominant color family.

2. Playfulness needs a serious canvas
   - Warm cream, graphite text, precise cards.
   - No random white background.
   - No drop-shadow clutter.

3. One dark CTA anchors the page
   - Near-black pill creates conversion focus.
   - Orange stays inline and illustrative, not filled button background.

4. Cards stay flat and tactile
   - Use inset warm-stone ring instead of shadows.
   - Keep product phone mockups contained.

5. Composition surrounds the headline
   - Characters flank and overlap the hero text.
   - The hero is not a split layout.

## Anti-patterns

- Mascots as random stickers.
- More than 4-5 illustration colors in one scene.
- Orange filled CTA competing with dark CTA.
- Pure white canvas.
- Generic rounded SaaS cards.
- Drop shadows on every feature card.
- Playful type for dense UI.
