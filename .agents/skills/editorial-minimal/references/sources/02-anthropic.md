# Anthropic - Editorial Minimal Source Notes

## Core read

Anthropic is the research-journal variant of editorial minimalism. It avoids pure
white and pure black, using warm ivory and slate instead. The design feels
institutional, literary, and serious. The main identity move is a sans/serif
pairing where sans handles navigation and UI, while serif supplies editorial
weight in feature cards and large content moments.

Decoration is replaced by typographic emphasis. Word-level underlines, not
colored highlights, carry attention. Large dark feature cards interrupt the warm
paper canvas like printed plates in a research publication.

## Typography

- Primary UI: Anthropic Sans or Inter / DM Sans fallback.
- Sans weights: 400, 500, 600, 700.
- Sans display: around 61px at 700 with -0.02em tracking.
- Sans body: 15-16px, slight negative tracking.
- Display/content: Anthropic Serif or Playfair Display / Lora fallback.
- Serif sizes: 18, 20, 24, 91.
- Serif weights: 400, 600.
- Mono: Anthropic Mono or JetBrains Mono / IBM Plex Mono.
- Mono role: metadata fields, category labels, structured data.

## Structure

- Page base: warm ivory (#faf9f5), never pure white.
- Strong dark feature cards: near-black #141413.
- Max width: around 1200px.
- Density: compact editorial.
- Section gap: around 61px.
- Card padding: around 31px.
- Element gaps: 8-16px.
- Card radii: 8px, 16px panels, 24px featured cards.
- Buttons mostly square/flat; one asymmetric CTA may be special.

## Component lessons

1. Thick keyword underline
   - Use underline as emphasis.
   - No color highlight, no badge, no bold jump.

2. Dark editorial feature card
   - Slate Dark fill.
   - Ivory text.
   - Serif heading.
   - 24px radius and hard image clipping.

3. Metadata footer
   - Mono labels.
   - Small structured fields like DATE and CATEGORY.
   - Signals research/archive credibility.

4. Flat navigation
   - Warm ivory surface.
   - Sans labels.
   - Minimal buttons.

5. Sparse accent use
   - Clay or other earthy accents at section level only.
   - No default saturated UI chroma.

## Design lessons

- Warm paper can replace white minimalism.
- Editorial authority can come from type pairing instead of color.
- Underline can be a signature decorative system.
- Dark cards can create rhythm without gradients or shadows.
- Mono metadata makes editorial content feel structured and research-grade.

## Anti-patterns

- Do not use pure white or pure black surfaces.
- Do not use colored highlight backgrounds.
- Do not use many accent colors.
- Do not turn all cards dark; dark cards should be strong editorial beats.
- Do not use serif everywhere; keep roles distinct.
- Do not add soft shadows or glass effects.
