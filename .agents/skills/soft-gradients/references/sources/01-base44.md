# Base44 - Soft Gradients Source Notes

## Core read

Base44 is the "soft AI canvas" archetype: almost-white surfaces, quiet product
structure, rounded but crisp cards, and gradients that feel like reflected
ambient light rather than decoration. It is not pastel in the childish sense.
The palette is mostly pearl, snow, graphite, and slate; lime and orange appear
as product signals.

The important skill is restraint. The gradient is there to warm the workspace,
not to carry every section. The interface still reads as a tool: clear nav,
direct CTAs, compact product modules, and functional typography.

## Typography

- Primary voice: modern sans, plain, legible, product-first.
- Headings can be large but should avoid ornamental weight.
- Body copy needs strong contrast against pearl backgrounds.
- Labels should be compact and functional, often 12-14px.
- Avoid decorative serif unless the page is intentionally editorial.
- Use sentence case for product copy.

## Structure

- Base unit: 4px.
- Density: comfortable but not sparse.
- Section gap: around 45-64px.
- Card padding: around 32-40px for major panels.
- Element gap: around 10-16px.
- Radius scale: 3px for precise controls, 7-10px for cards, 14px for larger
  containers, pills for tags.
- Backgrounds should preserve a large amount of unbroken off-white space.

## Component lessons

1. Pearl workspace
   - Use an off-white base instead of pure white.
   - Add white cards only when hierarchy needs an elevation change.
   - Make borders visible but soft.

2. AI-action accent
   - Lime is reserved for creation, generation, submit, or "active intelligence".
   - Do not use lime as broad background across the entire page.
   - Pair with graphite text to avoid low-contrast neon mistakes.

3. Warm secondary signal
   - Orange can mark "new", "template", "highlight", or "warning-lite".
   - Keep it as a chip, underline, badge, or small fill.

4. Soft gradient bands
   - Best used behind hero/product moments.
   - Keep opacity low enough that text remains sober.
   - Avoid diagonal rainbow intensity.

5. Compact product cards
   - Cards should feel usable, not like decorative tiles.
   - Include real affordances: tabs, list rows, inputs, toolbars, preview states.

## Reusable pattern

Use this source when a product needs to feel:

- AI-native but not sci-fi
- friendly but competent
- warm without beige overload
- clean without sterile blue-gray SaaS sameness
- softly luminous without blur-heavy glassmorphism

## Anti-patterns

- Lime everywhere.
- Pure white page with no warmth.
- Massive vague gradient hero with no product UI.
- Childish pastel blobs.
- Overly soft text contrast.
- Hero headline in ultra-heavy black weight.
- Rounded-card collage with no workflow.
